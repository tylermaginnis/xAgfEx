Getting Started

Step 1: Open PDF Redactor Pro

  1. Navigate to PDF Redactor Pro in your web browser

Step 2: Load Your PDF Document

Method 1: Using the Choose PDF Button

  1. Click the blue "Choose PDF" button in the main toolbar
  2. Select your PDF file from your computer
  3. Wait for the document to load and render

Method 2: Using the File Menu

  1. Click "File" in the menu bar

Creating Redactions

Basic Redaction Process

  1. Position your cursor over sensitive text or content
  2. Click and drag to select the area you want to redact
  3. Watch the red selection box appear as you drag
  4. Release the mouse to create the redaction
  5. See the redaction appear in the sidebar list

Understanding the Selection Process

  • Red overlay boxes show your selected areas
  • Real-time feedback as you drag your selection
  • Precise control - select exactly what you need
  • Multiple selections - add as many redactions as needed

Redaction Sidebar Management

Each redaction appears in the left sidebar showing:

  • Page number where the redaction is located
  • Coordinates of the redaction area
  • Delete button to remove individual redactions
  • Click to highlight - click any redaction to locate it in the document

Customizing Redaction Appearance

Using the Redaction Toolbar

Before or after creating redactions, customize their appearance:

  • Use the color picker to set redaction fill color

Border Color

  • Set the border color independently from the background
  • Helps define redaction boundaries clearly

Border Width

  • Adjust from 0-10 pixels
  • Thicker borders for more visibility
  • Thinner borders for subtle redactions

Opacity

  • Control transparency from 10-100%
  • Lower opacity to see underlying content

Applying Changes

  • Click "Apply to All Redactions" to update existing redactions
  • New redactions will use current settings automatically

  • Click the "X" button next to any redaction in the sidebar

Applying and Saving Your Work

Step 1: Apply Redactions

  1. Once you've selected all areas to redact, click "Apply Redactions"
  2. The system processes your document and applies permanent redactions
  3. Red selection boxes become solid black redaction blocks
  4. The "Save Redacted PDF" button becomes available

Step 2: Save Your Redacted Document

  1. Click "Save Redacted PDF" button

Best Practices for Professional Redaction

What to Redact

Personal Information

  • Names, addresses, phone numbers
  • Social Security numbers
  • Email addresses and personal identifiers

Financial Data

  • Account numbers, credit card information
  • Salary details, financial statements
  • Tax information

Medical Information

  • Patient names and identifiers
  • Health conditions and treatment details
  • Insurance information

Legal Information

  • Attorney-client privileged communications
  • Case-specific sensitive details
  • Settlement amounts and terms

Redaction Strategies

Be Thorough

  • Review the entire document, including headers and footers
  • Check for information that might identify individuals indirectly
  • Consider context clues that might reveal redacted information

Be Consistent

  • Use the same redaction style throughout your document
  • Apply consistent rules for what information to redact
  • Maintain professional appearance

Verify Your Work

  • Review each page after creating redactions
  • Use the sidebar list to check all redaction locations
  • Apply redactions and review the final result before saving

Troubleshooting Common Issues

Document Won't Load

  • Ensure your PDF isn't password-protected
  • Check that the file isn't corrupted
  • Try refreshing the page and loading again

Redactions Aren't Precise

  • Zoom in on the area for more precise selection
  • Make smaller, more targeted selections
  • Use multiple small redactions instead of one large one

Can't See Redactions

  • Check that opacity isn't set too low
  • Ensure border width is visible
  • Try changing the redaction color for better contrast

Keyboard Shortcuts and Tips

Productivity Tips

Efficient Selection

  • Start selections slightly outside the text you want to redact
  • Make selections slightly larger than needed to ensure complete coverage
  • Use consistent sizing for similar types of information

Organization

  • Work page by page for systematic coverage
  • Use the sidebar to track your progress
  • Group similar types of redactions together
